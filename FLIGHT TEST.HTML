The cowling has been removed for some flight tests. Our C172 engine kit is currently completing high altitude and winter flight operations in the Rocky Mountains. Items such as starting a cold soaked engine in subzero ambient temperatures and assuring engine temperatures remain normal during extended idle descents are demonstrated. Also, multiple engine failures are induced to assure quick in-flight restarts by the auxiliary (AUX) EFI system even in freezing conditions. This Video Simulates an electrical failure of the normal electrical system and main EFI system; at 50 feet and Vx speed, the main system is completely de-powered, causing immediate engine failure. Within less than 1 second, the AUX system returns the engine to normal operating condition; you may notice a brief change in prop speed at time of failure. The red flashing alert on the SED is displaying the fault description and specific codes associated with the engine failure, the yellow flashing lamp indicates the AUX system is powering the engine.
